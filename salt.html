what's the show?

A one-person comedy from a guy who joined the Navy to meet guys.

I grew up in Salt Lake — the only city in America where being a Jew makes you a gentile — came out in 2000 right as Mitt Romney was saving the Winter Olympics, and graduated West High in 2003. After a brief stint with the Howard Dean and John Kerry campaigns, I did what any sensible gay teen would: I enlisted in the U.S. Navy.

I spent four years in the Western Pacific aboard USS Patriot, an Avenger-class minesweeper, visiting more than 30 countries on a mission to clear mines and, you know, meet people.

Twink or Swim is the show I've been writing in my head since 2004. It's about being closeted in a closet that's also a boat. It's about Don't Ask, Don't Tell. It's about how the Navy is hilarious if you survive it.

"I joined the Navy and spent four years on the crew of a 224-foot warship made out of wood — the boat, and the Sailors, too." — me, in the show
why I'm losing my mind

Three reasons I can't shut up about Fringe.

01

It's a homecoming

I performed my first-ever stand-up set in 2012 at Wiseguys at Trolley Square — about a hundred feet from the Alliance Theatre, where Fringe lives. Coming back to do an hour of comedy in the same building feels enormous.

02

It's the right room

Fringe shows are original, uncensored, and unadjudicated. That's exactly the kind of room a one-person show about being gay in the military needs. No notes, no committee, no asterisks.

03

It's Salt Lake

I want to tell this story to the city that raised the kid who joined the Navy. Salt Lake audiences are smart, generous, and they've seen things. I'm bringing my whole chest.

about the artist

J'myle Koretz, in roughly his own words.

J'myle Koretz is a Seattle-based stand-up comedian. He has hosted and featured at Tacoma Comedy Club and Emerald City Comedy Club (Seattle), and was selected twice for The Hollywood Comedy Store's Potluck Show. He performs regularly from San Francisco to Vancouver.

He has studied under Alice Fraser (The Bugle), Tiff Stevenson (HBO's Avenue 5), and Andrew Frank (Great Salt Lake Fringe, 2022). His first stand-up set was at Wiseguys Comedy Club's Trolley Square location in 2012 — about a hundred feet from the Alliance Theatre.

The Short Version Salt Lake → West High '03 → Dean/Kerry campaigns → U.S. Navy ('03–'07, USS Patriot, 30+ countries) → Seattle → comedy clubs from SF to Vancouver → back to SLC for Fringe 2026.
